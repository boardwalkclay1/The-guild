THE GRAND GLOSSARY

A complete Codex of the market and the Guild. Real stock language first. Metaphors and myth last.

CORE MARKET FOUNDATIONS (A–Z)

Ask

Origin: Market Basics Symbolizes: What sellers are asking for
The ask is the price sellers are currently willing to accept for a stock or option. It’s the “offer” side of the market.

Bank

Origin: Accessing Options Symbolizes: Where your trading money starts
Your bank is where your cash lives before it reaches your broker. You move money from your bank into your brokerage account to trade.

See Banks Module

Bid

Origin: Market Basics Symbolizes: What buyers are willing to pay
The bid is the price buyers are currently willing to pay for a stock or option. It’s the “demand” side of the market.

Broker

Origin: Accessing Options Symbolizes: Your weapon of execution
A broker is the platform that connects you to the market and lets you place trades. It holds your trading account, routes your orders, and shows you quotes and charts.

Visit Brokers Chamber

Candlestick

Origin: Chart Basics Symbolizes: One unit of price story
A candlestick shows how price moved during a specific time period: open, high, low, and close. The body and wicks tell you who was in control — buyers or sellers.

Chart

Origin: Chart Patterns Symbolizes: The battlefield map
A chart is a visual record of price over time. It’s where you read trends, levels, patterns, and behavior.

Chart Pattern

Origin: Pattern Modules Symbolizes: The language of price
A chart pattern is a repeated shape in price action that hints at what might happen next — continuation, reversal, or indecision.

View Pattern Training

Contract

Origin: Options Chain Symbolizes: One options position
An options contract is an agreement that gives you the right, but not the obligation, to buy or sell 100 shares of a stock at a specific price before a specific date.

Equity

Origin: Market Basics Symbolizes: Ownership value
Equity is ownership value — either in a company (stock) or in your account (account equity). It’s what you actually own after gains and losses.

Expiration

Origin: Options Chain Symbolizes: The deadline
Expiration is the last day an options contract is valid. After this date, the contract either expires worthless or is exercised/assigned.

In The Money (ITM)

Origin: Golden Rules / Options Symbolizes: Contracts with real value
An option is in the money when the stock price is already beyond the strike in your favor. ITM contracts have intrinsic value and usually move cleaner with the stock.

Study ITM vs OTM

Limit Order

Origin: Order Types Symbolizes: Precision entry or exit
A limit order tells your broker to buy or sell only at a specific price or better. It gives you control over price, but not a guarantee of execution.

Liquidity

Origin: Market Mechanics Symbolizes: How easily you can get in and out
Liquidity is how easily you can buy or sell without moving the price too much. High liquidity = tighter spreads and smoother fills.

Margin Account

Origin: Account Types Symbolizes: Borrowed firepower
A margin account lets you borrow money from your broker to trade larger size. It increases both your potential gains and your potential losses.

Market Order

Origin: Order Types Symbolizes: Speed over precision
A market order tells your broker to buy or sell immediately at the best available price. You get speed, but not exact control over the fill price.

Open Interest

Origin: Options Chain Symbolizes: How many contracts exist
Open interest is the number of active options contracts that exist for a specific strike and expiration. Higher open interest usually means better liquidity.

Option

Origin: Options Training Symbolizes: Leverage on a stock
An option is a contract that derives its value from an underlying stock. It gives you the right, but not the obligation, to buy or sell that stock at a set price before a set date.

Access Options Path

Premium

Origin: Options Chain Symbolizes: The cost of the contract
Premium is the price you pay (or receive) for an options contract. It reflects time, volatility, and how far the strike is from the current stock price.

Share

Origin: Market Basics Symbolizes: One unit of ownership
A share is one unit of ownership in a company. When you buy shares, you own a piece of that business.

Spread

Origin: Market Mechanics Symbolizes: The cost of entering
The spread is the difference between the bid and the ask. Wider spreads usually mean lower liquidity and higher friction.

Stock

Origin: Market Basics Symbolizes: Ownership in a company
A stock is a piece of ownership in a company. When you buy stock, you’re buying a slice of that company’s future.

Ticker

Origin: Market Basics Symbolizes: The stock’s name in code
A ticker is the short symbol used to identify a stock (like AAPL, TSLA, SPY). It’s how you find and trade a specific company or ETF.

Volume

Origin: Market Mechanics Symbolizes: How loud the market is speaking
Volume is the number of shares traded during a specific period. High volume means strong participation and conviction.
TECHNICAL ANALYSIS: LEVELS & PATTERNS

Support

Origin: Support & Resistance Symbolizes: The floor
Support is a price level where buyers have stepped in before and may step in again. It acts like a floor where price often bounces.

Study Support & Resistance

Resistance

Origin: Support & Resistance Symbolizes: The ceiling
Resistance is a price level where sellers have stepped in before and may step in again. It acts like a ceiling where price often stalls or reverses.

Look Left

Origin: Training Hall Symbolizes: Respect for history
“Look left” means scroll back on the chart to see where price has reacted before. The past reveals the levels that still matter today.

Learn the Look Left Rule

Even Numbers

Origin: Golden Rules / Training Hall Symbolizes: Collective focus points
Even numbers (like 100, 150, 200) attract attention. Many traders watch them, so reactions there are often bigger and cleaner.

Study Even Numbers
MOVING AVERAGES CATALOG

Moving Average (MA)

Origin: Moving Averages Module Symbolizes: Smoothed trend
A moving average is the average price over a set number of periods. It smooths out noise and shows the underlying trend.

Enter Moving Averages Module

9 MA

Origin: Moving Averages Symbolizes: Very short-term momentum
The 9-period moving average tracks very short-term momentum. It’s often used for quick scalps and intraday direction.

20 MA

Origin: Moving Averages Symbolizes: Short-term trend
The 20-period moving average shows the short-term trend. Many traders watch it for pullbacks and bounces.

50 MA

Origin: Moving Averages Symbolizes: Intermediate trend
The 50-period moving average tracks the intermediate trend. It’s a key line for swing traders and bigger moves.

100 MA

Origin: Moving Averages Symbolizes: Longer-term structure
The 100-period moving average shows a deeper trend. It often acts as a strong support or resistance on higher timeframes.

200 MA

Origin: Moving Averages Symbolizes: Major trend line
The 200-period moving average is a major line watched by institutions and long-term traders. Above it often means long-term strength; below it often means long-term weakness.
CHART PATTERNS CATALOG

Double Bottom

Origin: Chart Patterns Symbolizes: Potential bottoming
A double bottom is a pattern where price tests a low twice and holds. It often signals that sellers are exhausted and buyers are stepping in.

Double Top

Origin: Chart Patterns Symbolizes: Potential topping
A double top is a pattern where price tests a high twice and fails. It often signals that buyers are exhausted and sellers are taking control.

Rounding Bottom

Origin: Chart Patterns Symbolizes: Slow accumulation
A rounding bottom is a smooth, curved bottom that forms over time. It often signals a slow shift from selling to buying.

Flag

Origin: Chart Patterns Symbolizes: Pause before continuation
A flag is a small consolidation after a strong move. It often breaks in the direction of the original move.

Trend Break

Origin: Chart Patterns Symbolizes: Shift in control
A trend break happens when price breaks out of a trendline or channel. It often signals a change in direction or a pause in the current trend.
OPTIONS LANGUAGE

Call Option

Origin: Options Basics Symbolizes: Bullish leverage
A call option gives you the right, but not the obligation, to buy 100 shares of a stock at a specific price (strike) before expiration.

Put Option

Origin: Options Basics Symbolizes: Bearish leverage or protection
A put option gives you the right, but not the obligation, to sell 100 shares of a stock at a specific price (strike) before expiration.

Strike Price

Origin: Options Chain Symbolizes: The line in the sand
The strike price is the price at which you can buy (call) or sell (put) the stock if you exercise the option.
